Verdades Latentes | Data viz


Latent Truths is an interactive visualization and sound project that explores the human impact of the armed conflict in Colombia using data on victims of antipersonnel mines. The project utilizes information from the Open Data Platform of the Observatory of Memory and Conflict, transforming statistical records into an immersive sensory experience.

The project aims to shift data from the realm of information to the realm of feeling, revealing the stories and impacts hidden behind seemingly abstract figures. Through generative visualizations, sound, and rhythmic pulses inspired by heartbeats, Latent Truths constructs a space where the viewer's body becomes an active participant in interpreting the data.

The project's technical development integrates tools such as TouchDesigner and Arduino, which allow for the translation of patterns and relationships present in the data into real-time visual and auditory stimuli. Each heartbeat serves as a reminder of the fragility of life and the enduring consequences of conflict, inviting an empathetic and critical reading of the archive.

Latent Truths proposes a memory device: a space for reflection, mourning and dialogue, where technology seeks to make pain perceptible without reducing it to a number.
