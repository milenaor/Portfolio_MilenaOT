ChatBot:Civibot | Retrieval-Augmented Generation


Civibot is an artificial intelligence project developed to explore new ways of accessing, reading, and interpreting institutional files through conversational interfaces.

The project is based on activating an archive of approximately 2,000 documents related to citizenship, consisting of reports, guidelines, initiatives, and other public content. To make it searchable, the archive underwent a prior process of cleaning, normalizing, and structuring the data, using a tool developed specifically for the project, which integrates artificial intelligence to process large volumes of documentary information.

This tool transformed a heterogeneous set of files into a machine-readable corpus, identifying relevant categories, themes, relationships, and metadata. Based on this, Civibot enables a conversational interface that allows users to ask questions in natural language, accessing the content without needing to know its internal structure.

From a digital humanities perspective, Civibot conceives of the archive as a living territory, open to exploration, questioning, and reinterpretation. The bot allows for the identification of patterns, recurring themes, relationships, and gaps within the corpus, adding value to both academic processes and institutional decision-making.

The development of Civibot integrated:
-Processing, cleaning, and structuring a large-scale document corpus.
-Development of a proprietary AI-connected tool for data preparation.
-Language models for semantic querying and information synthesis.
-Conversation-centered user experience design.
-User testing to evaluate comprehension, clarity, and usability.
